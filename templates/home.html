Addressing Challenges in Premix Fuel

Government subsidized the product to make it cheap and accessible to artisanal fishermen in the coastal and lake regions of the country. Majority of Ghanaians who live along the coast are fishermen and engage in the fishing business, therefore, depriving them of the premix fuel is as good as starving them and their dependents. Ever since the product was introduced into the fishing industry, its distribution has always been fraught with issues of corruption, diversions and smuggling to neighbouring destinations, which defeated the rationale of the subsidy and made the commodity costly to fishermen. PREMIX Fuel Distribution To Go Digital

Vice President Dr Mahamudu Bawumia has noted that premix fuel meant for fishermen are being diverted to retailers by unscrupulous persons as a result of the heavy subsidies on the fuel. Dr Bawumia said the issue of premix fuel diversions has been a major concern to the government over the years. He has therefore asked the Minister of Fisheries and Aquaculture, Mavis Hawa Koomson to deal with the issue immediately. Read More ....

Fuel Distribution Is Being Bigitized By The PREMIX Secretariat.

The National Premix Fuel Secretariat (NPFS) has launched a digitisation drive to increase transparency and prevent leaks in the premix fuel trade. According to the Chairman of the National Premix Committee, Nii Lante Bannerman, only fishermen with electronic cards will be able to access the product under the new drive.
